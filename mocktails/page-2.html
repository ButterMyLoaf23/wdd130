Shaking is a crucial step for many cocktails, and it's faster than stirring because the ingredients are repeatedly exposed to the ice. When shaking, it's best to use large ice cubes or chunks, as they melt more slowly than crushed ice and won't over-dilute the drink.

This is a Jigger. This helps measure and ensure consistent drink flavors and prevents drinks from being too strong or too weak.

A muddler is an essential bar tool designed to smash and mix drink ingredients. Essentially, a bartender's pestle, using a muddler is one of the best ways to infuse the flavor of fresh fruits and herbs into your drinks.

A cocktail strainer is important because it prevents unwanted ingredients from ending up in the drink, and ensures the cocktail has a consistent texture and is visually appealing.

A cocktail spoon, also known as a bar spoon or stirrer, is a key tool for making cocktails because it allows for smooth stirring and mixing, and can also be used for layering and garnishing.

The main function of a pourer is to provide a controlled and consistent flow of liquid when pouring from a bottle. By using a pourer, you can reduce spills and drips that can occur when pouring directly from a bottle without a controlled spout.
